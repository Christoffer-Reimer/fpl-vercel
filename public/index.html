How FPL price changes work

When prices rise

A player's price rises when more managers buy than sell. The threshold is roughly net +1,000 transfers. Prices rise by £0.1m at a time.

Selling profit rule

If you bought a player at £7.0m and they're now £7.4m, you sell for £7.2m — you keep half the rise, rounded down to the nearest £0.1m.

Strategy tip

Buy rising players early — before the price moves. Sell before a run of hard fixtures to lock in profit. Never sell just after a price rise if another rise looks likely.

Before buying a player

✓
Good upcoming fixtures?
Check the next 5 GWs. Avoid buying into a hard run.
✓
Reliable starter?
Target 85%+ minutes in recent weeks. Rotation risk kills value.
✓
Form real or a one-off?
Look at underlying stats (shots, key passes) not just points.
✓
Price fair for the output?
Target 6+ pts per game for a £8m+ player.
!
Buying because of last week?
If yes, wait 24 hours and check again. This is the panic trap.
!
Will they be highly owned?
40%+ ownership means missing them is a big risk — but so is everyone blanking together.
✗
Don't buy into a difficult fixture
Even great players struggle vs top defences.
✗
Don't overpay for potential
A £12m player needs 8+ pts/GW to justify the cost.

Sell vs hold decision

✓
Sell if: fixtures get significantly harder
3+ difficult games in a row is a strong sell signal.
✓
Sell if: lost their starting place
Rotation or injury that looks long-term.
✓
Sell if: price risen and better value exists
Use the profit to upgrade elsewhere.
!
One bad week is not a sell signal
Check if underlying stats still look good.
!
Don't sell during a good fixture run
Even patchy form + easy fixtures = potential.
✗
Don't sell just because they blanked
Top players blank regularly. It's variance, not decline.
✗
Don't sell captain before a big fixture
Sell after the good run, not before it.

Premium player criteria (£9m+)

✓
Must average 7+ pts per game
Premiums need to deliver consistently to justify the budget.
✓
Must be captain material most weeks
If you wouldn't captain them, they're probably not worth the premium cost.
✓
Buy early and hold all season
Rotating premiums is expensive. Your Haaland/Salah churn cost you this season.
!
Max 2–3 premiums in your squad
More than 3 leaves your bench dangerously thin.
✗
Don't sell a premium after one blank
Salah and Haaland will always return. Hold through blank weeks.

Differential hunting (sub 15% owned)

✓
Form + easy fixtures = key signal
The combination of both is the strongest differential indicator.
✓
Target set piece takers at budget clubs
Penalty/free kick takers at £5–6m are gold dust.
✓
Watch transfer activity
Rising transfers in = the crowd is spotting something. Don't chase too late.
!
Differentials are higher risk
Only use when you need rank ground, not when protecting a good rank.
✗
Don't pick differentials just to be different
There needs to be a data-backed reason, not just low ownership.

The 24-hour rule — your most important habit for 26/27

Step 1 — After a bad GW

Close FPL. Do not open the transfer page. Write down which players frustrated you but don't act yet.

Step 2 — Next day

Check fixtures for the next 5 GWs. Look at underlying stats (xG, xA). Is the frustration still justified by the data?

Step 3 — Then decide

If the data supports the transfer, make it. If it was just a bad week, hold. Most of the time you'll hold — and that's the right call.
